What is Agent Based Modeling?

Agent Based Modeling (ABM) is a tool for simulating natural and social phenomena, from the spread of wildfire to the ups and downs in the stock market.

It has a ton of applications, including:

  • in biology, to study predator/prey dynamics, coordination in ants and other super-organisms, and the immune system
  • in ecology and land stewardship, to understand how fire, rain, erosion, and vegetation all effect each other
  • in epidemiology, to study the spread of infection
  • in urban planning, to simulate traffic congestion and evacuation
  • in economics, to study financial markets and game theory
  • in computer graphics, to animate schools of fish, flocks of birds, particle effects, and crowds

People use agent-based models to understand the underlying principles of phenomena that have no central brain or authority.

The basic idea is to give simple rules to a bunch of agents in an environment, let them loose, and see what happens!

What is Agentscript?

Agentscript is a javascript library for writing agent based models that run in your web browser. It is inspired by an incredible language called Netlogo.
